Separating myth from reality in shark conservation

The Misconception

When most people think of sharks, they imagine danger‚Äîa menacing predator lurking beneath the waves. Popular media has painted sharks as humanity's aquatic adversary, but the data tells a profoundly different story.

Through comprehensive analysis of global shark populations, human-shark interactions, and fishing mortality data, we reveal an uncomfortable truth: while we fear sharks, they have far more reason to fear us. This project examines the real numbers behind shark conservation and challenges the narrative that has dominated our cultural consciousness for decades.
